About Me

The aim of the study was to evaluate the Effectiveness of Brainfeed Alternative Intervention Programme on Improvement processing-speed of Students Living with Dyslexia in Port Harcourt metropolis Rivers State. Five research questions and five null Hypotheses guided this study. Quasi experimental design by Solomon Four-group design was used in the study. Gibson test of cognitive skills was used as pretest and post-test. Sixty students formed the sample in the study. The research questions were answered using mean, standard deviation of the pretest and post-test scores, while dependent and independent samples t-test, one way and two way analysis of variance (ANOVA) were used to analyse the null Hypotheses. The result proved that the Brainfeed alternative intervention program can enhance processing-speed of students living with dyslexia. Recommendations were made Based on the findings of this study
